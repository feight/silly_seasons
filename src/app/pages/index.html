Synopsis

Silly Seasons is an animated CG comedy featuring the escapades of four girls, Daisy, Summer, Ginger and Snowflake, who all live in the town of Sillyville with a supporting cast of offbeat animals and flowers.  The 26 x 24' series is a lighthearted and irreverent take on relationships and life in general; the show is aimed at 8-12 y.o. girls.

The four girls are the only humans in the series as well as the only kids. All the other characters - anthropomorphic animals and plants - are adults. This forms the foundation for comedy, as the series parodies adult behavior and attitudes towards kids in a fun and humorous way.

The Girls

Daisy

Daisy is a self-proclaimed fashionista and highly sophisticated socialite. She is committed to maintaining aesthetic standards for the whole of Sillyville.

Summer

Summer is the brainiac of the four girls. She spends her considerable mental resources trying to resolve conflict and finding the safest course of action for the girls.

Ginger

Ginger does really know what is best for everyone - all the time. And she is not afraid to say it, loudly and repeatedly. Ginger also loves sports, as long as she can win.

Snowflake

Snowflake is a connoisseur of all things hot and chocolatey. Devoted to the delicious and fantastical, her irrepressible optimism carries her unscathed through the craziest of adventures.

The Cast

Cam

Cam is part genius inventor, part mad scientist with a deep penchant for the dramatic. Cam’s attempts at solving Sillyville’s problems often lead the girls into hilarious and silly predicaments. 

Bolt

Bolt is Cam's robot sidekick, companion and whipping boy who is mercilessly blamed for much that goes wrong in Cam's life - and there is a lot that goes wrong. Bolt is voiceless and communicates via ideograms burnt onto pieces of toast that pop out of his head, which is a modified toaster.

Vlad

Polar bear, baker and proprietor of Vlad's Patisserie is a rock in the stormy seas of Sillyville. He can be found behind the counter at the patisserie dispensing advice and hot chocolate.

Deadpan

Deadpan is Sillyville's Machiavellian mayor - an orangutan who makes Sillyville his own personal fiefdom to do with just what he pleases until his own greed and avarice inevitably torpedoes his own underhanded schemes.

Ron

Ron is the brainy member of Sillyvision's anchor duo. Ron's job is mostly keeping his partner, Don, from ruining the news. 

Don

Don is the brainless half of Sillyville's news anchor team. Don blunders through news broadcasts and interviews, oblivious to how the news doesn't always have to be about him.

Venus

Venus is Silly Seasons' resident roving reporter and host of the 'Flower Power Half Hour'. Gossip is her middle name and nothing stands in the way of Venus and a delicious scoop, not even the facts.

Harry

Harry is the resident deliveryman. Kind and always on time, well known among all Sillyvilians as the happy hippo, Harry. 

Scary

Scary is a terrifyingly cute, under-the-bed-monster. Found by Daisy, this little guy is petrified of everything around him. He also suffers from acute self-esteem issues, and as a result, had become Daisy's pet project. 

Gerald

Gerald is Snowflake's accommodating butler. Trained at the South Pole Penguin Academy, he aims to please and prides himself in his pristine delivery and appearance. Nothing is too big or too small for Gerald to deal with, especially if it's for his dear Miss Snowflake. 

Spike

Spike is Sillyville Mayor Deadpan's loyal assistant. Spike's unsurpassed loyalty to Deadpan informs all that he does. More than anything he wants Deadpan's approval and will stop at nothing to get it.

Cameron

Cameron is Cam's overblown windbag cousin. He's always done more, gone further or flown higher than anyone else, but can never be pinned down on the exact details of his exploits.

Camilla

Camilla is Cameron's unfortunate life partner. Almost everything that this feisty lady-chameleon does is to foil or expose Cameron. She loves to tease Cameron and seems to warm to anyone but him, however, their relationship endures despite them both.

About the Flying Circus

The Flying Circus produces CG animated kids-comedy using a state of the art motion capture system.  Our production philosophy utilizes experienced physical theatre performers, and as a result, the process delivers a fresh and spontaneous style of animation with fast and snappy dialogue.  Improvisation between the actors and the 'live' nature of the performances gives a unique feel to the animation and allows a large degree of flexibility during production.   The advantage of a motion capture-based production process is its ability to quickly turn around multiple episodes once all the 3D assets are developed while still retaining high production values.
